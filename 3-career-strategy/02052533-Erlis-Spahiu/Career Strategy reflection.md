1. What surprised you the most? 

->Organizational Lag: Hands down, learning how quickly individuals are to jump on the latest tech wagon vs how slow organizations adapt.

->Training Reality: It was shocking to learn how few companies actually provide mentorship, technical direction, or training to keep up with the speed. It really is the days of expecting your company to guide your learning path are gone.

2. What worries you about AI in software engineering?

->Accelerating Skill Gap: AI is making its way into software development lifecycle at breakneck speeds abstracting away boilerplate code and rote technical tasks.

->Surface Level Engineers: My biggest fear is engineers taking shortcuts with these tools and not taking the time to learn the high level inner workings of what they build. AI has the potential to widen the skill gap exponentially if we don't use our time wisely. It's easy to become complacent and suddenly you're doing the same things for years.

3. What opportunities do you see? 

->Leave the Mundane to AI: Because AI is taking over the manual code generation, we as engineers have more brain space than ever before.

->Elevate your game: Shift focus from memorizing pointless syntax to working on high value tasks like architecture, output verification, and elegant design.
