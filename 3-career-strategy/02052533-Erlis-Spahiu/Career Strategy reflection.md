1. What surprised you the most? 

-> Hands down, learning how quickly individuals are to jump on the latest tech wagon vs how slow organizations adapt.

-> It was shocking to learn how few companies actually provide mentorship, technical direction, or training to keep up with the speed. It really is the days of expecting your company to guide your learning path are gone.

2. What worries you about AI in software engineering?

-> AI is making its way into software development lifecycle at breakneck speeds abstracting away boilerplate code and rote technical tasks.

-> My biggest fear is engineers taking shortcuts with these tools and not taking the time to learn the high level inner workings of what they build. AI has the potential to widen the skill gap exponentially if we don't use our time wisely. It's easy to become complacent and suddenly you're doing the same things for years.

3. What opportunities do you see? 

->Because AI is taking over the manual code generation, we as engineers have more brain space than ever before.

-> Shifting focus from memorizing pointless syntax to working on high value tasks like architecture, output verification, and elegant design.

4. What Skills will weather the storm?

-> Skills that are language/framework agnostic will weather the storm. 

-> More specifically skills such as system design, output verification, and collaborating with people around the world. Skills that cannot be easily replaced by software.

5. How will software engineers need to learn differently in the future?

-> Engineers will need to learn in a nonlinear fashion. Blending online, bite-sized, AI-generated lessons with the classic human mentors of hands-on project work.

-> Force yourself to read other people’s code. Get used to open global peer code review. Learn how other professionals think about solving problems at scale.

6. Did any of your opinions change after conducting your research? If so, what?

->Individual vs. Company? : Yes. I used to believe that if you didn’t keep up with technology, it was solely on you to hustle. Study more. Learn more libraries. Frameworks.

-> My research changed my mind that an individual can not battle this time constraint. I believe companies with willpower to establish psychological safety, allow internal switching, and promote a failure forgiving culture will have long-term retention.

7. How will YOU personally evolve?

->Instead of panicking about memorizing docs of every new technology framework I learn, I will focus on deeply understanding architectural patterns.

->I will lean on AI technology to help me take care of boiler-plate tasks where appropriate, but carefully review the dumps and use my time savings to learn how things work behind the scenes in the software I create.

->I will seek out open-source communities and employers who value collaboration & growth just as much as productivity.
